# LOW-TRUST SOURCE CATALOG

- `TRUST_LEVEL: LOW`
- `USE: BRAINSTORMING ONLY`
- `NOT_FOR: DIRECT EVIDENCE, RANKING, OR THESIS CHANGES`
- `REQUIRES: HIGH-QUALITY CONFIRMATION`

This file lists lower-trust but potentially high-value idea sources for Peras.

They are useful for:

- spotting weak early signals
- finding candidate markets or startup clusters
- generating product or investment hypotheses

They are not sufficient for:

- direct ranking changes
- thesis upgrades or downgrades
- investment-grade confidence

## Independent Frontier / Market Reports

- `State of AI Report`
  - URL: https://www.stateof.ai/
  - Why useful: independent yearly synthesis across research, commercialization, geopolitics, and infra
  - Risk note: high quality, but still a curated interpretation rather than a neutral source of record

- `SemiAnalysis`
  - URL: https://semianalysis.com/
  - Why useful: unusually strong independent analysis on semis, compute, data centers, energy, and AI infra
  - Risk note: excellent for signal generation, but still analyst-driven and not a primary market database

- `Artificial Analysis`
  - URL: https://www.artificialanalysis.ai/
  - Why useful: independent benchmark and model-pricing surface for frontier-model competition
  - Risk note: methodology can be useful, but it is still a third-party interpretive platform

- `Exploding Topics`
  - URL: https://explodingtopics.com/
  - Why useful: trend discovery, startup surfaces, and emerging-topic hints
  - Risk note: trend heuristics are not the same as durable value

## Independent Newsletters / Builder Commentary

- `Latent.Space`
  - URL: https://www.latent.space/
  - Why useful: strong engineering and agent/product signal from builders close to the tool layer
  - Risk note: builder-community bias and novelty bias

- `Ben's Bites`
  - URL: https://www.bensbites.com/
  - Why useful: wide-angle scan of AI products, tools, startups, and builder chatter
  - Risk note: high volume and commercial surface bias; useful for discovery, not ranking

- `Import AI`
  - URL: https://importai.substack.com/
  - Why useful: long-running independent view on AI progress and industry implications
  - Risk note: single-editor interpretation layer

- `Last Week in AI`
  - URL: https://www.lastweekin.ai/
  - Why useful: broad weekly curation across research, products, safety, and startups
  - Risk note: summary-heavy and vulnerable to selection bias

- `Interconnects`
  - URL: https://www.interconnects.ai/
  - Why useful: useful interpretive bridge between technical developments and industry movement
  - Risk note: independent and thoughtful, but still one authorial frame

- `Simon Willison's Weblog`
  - URL: https://simonwillison.net/
  - Why useful: catches practical model/tool/runtime changes early
  - Risk note: great for tooling signals, weaker for macro market inference

## Startup / Company Databases

- `Crunchbase`
  - URL: https://www.crunchbase.com/
  - Why useful: startup, funding, and ecosystem visibility
  - Risk note: partial data, ranking heuristics, and opaque coverage gaps

- `Dealroom`
  - URL: https://dealroom.co/
  - Why useful: startup ecosystems, funding, geography, and company tracking
  - Risk note: useful but not a complete or neutral global map

- `Product Hunt`
  - URL: https://www.producthunt.com/
  - Why useful: catches product launches and maker activity earlier than formal market reports
  - Risk note: heavy launch-audience bias and hype sensitivity

- `Wellfound`
  - URL: https://wellfound.com/
  - Why useful: startup activity and jobs surface
  - Risk note: incomplete and startup-self-report dependent

## Community / Social Discovery Surfaces

- `Hacker News`
  - URL: https://news.ycombinator.com/
  - Why useful: early builder and founder attention shifts
  - Risk note: very noisy, novelty-driven, and skewed toward certain technical cultures

- `Reddit r/MachineLearning`
  - URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/
  - Why useful: catches community reaction to papers, repos, and tools
  - Risk note: mixed quality and uneven signal-to-noise

- `Reddit r/LocalLLaMA`
  - URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/LocalLLaMA/
  - Why useful: early open-model, inference, and tooling enthusiasm signals
  - Risk note: extremely hype-sensitive and over-indexed on enthusiast priorities

- `Indie Hackers`
  - URL: https://www.indiehackers.com/
  - Why useful: bottoms-up product building and monetization clues
  - Risk note: biased toward small-tool entrepreneurship and anecdotal success

## Venture / Product Interpretation Surfaces

- `Stratechery`
  - URL: https://stratechery.com/
  - Why useful: independent strategic analysis on platform shifts and company structure
  - Risk note: argument-driven and selective rather than comprehensive

- `a16z AI`
  - URL: https://a16z.com/ai/
  - Why useful: useful venture-facing product and market hypotheses
  - Risk note: investor agenda and portfolio incentives must be assumed

- `Menlo Ventures AI`
  - URL: https://menlovc.com/perspectives/artificial-intelligence/
  - Why useful: startup and market framing for venture-relevant trends
  - Risk note: investor bias and thesis-shaping incentives

- `Sequoia AI`
  - URL: https://www.sequoiacap.com/companies/ai/
  - Why useful: useful for mapping what major investors want founders to build
  - Risk note: not neutral; should be read as strategic narrative, not truth

## Hiring / Talent Signal Surfaces

- `AI Engineer Summit / Latent Space event pages`
  - URL: https://www.latent.space/
  - Why useful: indicates where practical builder energy is clustering
  - Risk note: event communities can over-represent short-term trends

- `LinkedIn jobs and talent search surfaces`
  - URL: https://www.linkedin.com/jobs/
  - Why useful: rough demand signal for emerging roles and stack needs
  - Risk note: platform search bias and duplicate listings distort raw interpretation

## Working Rule

Any Peras note citing this catalog must explicitly say:

- the source is heuristic only
- the source is not treated as direct evidence
- thesis or ranking changes require confirmation from higher-trust source classes
