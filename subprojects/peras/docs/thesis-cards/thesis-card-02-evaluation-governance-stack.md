# Thesis Card 02 - Evaluation, Observability, And Governance Stack

## Title

Evaluation, observability, and governance systems for agentic AI

## Direction

Products that help organizations measure, supervise, audit, and govern AI
systems once those systems move beyond one-off chat interactions into workflows
and delegated action.

## Summary

As AI systems become more agentic, enterprises will need an entire trust and
control layer around them.

That makes the evaluation / observability / governance stack a strong
candidate direction.

## Why Now

- organizations are moving from experimentation toward operational deployment
- agentic systems increase the surface area for failure, drift, and compliance pressure
- enterprises need evidence, not just model access

## Frontier Signal

- agent framing is becoming central to frontier AI product and research language
- more AI work is moving toward tool use, delegation, and workflows

## Commercial Signal

- governance and audit tooling often becomes mandatory once AI leaves the lab
- enterprise AI buyers increasingly ask for reliability and oversight surfaces

## Historical Pattern

In major software waves, control and observability layers often become durable
value capture points once systems move into production.

## Upside Case

This layer can become foundational infrastructure across many downstream AI
applications.

## Bottlenecks

- proving ROI before governance budgets fully mature
- fragmented standards
- difficulty of building metrics that users trust

## Counterarguments

- some of this functionality may be absorbed by platforms
- buyers may delay spending until failures become more visible

## Confidence Level

Medium

## Evidence Class

- frontier lab agent framing
- enterprise AI policy and economic framing
- market need for auditability and deployment governance
