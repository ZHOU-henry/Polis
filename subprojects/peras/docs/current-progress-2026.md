# Peras Current Progress 2026

## 1. Academic Progress

- Open research infrastructure is rich enough that frontier signal can now be
  tracked at scale.
- Review surfaces, metadata graphs, and benchmark archives have made it easier
  to watch technical movement before it becomes mainstream product narrative.
- The strongest remaining problem is synthesis rather than collection.

## 2. Commercial / Industrial Progress

- Frontier labs publish at a pace that now matters for market timing, not only
  research curiosity.
- Open model ecosystems and hosted evaluation tools are compressing the gap
  between research release and product experimentation.
- Investors, founders, and operators increasingly need ranking layers, not more
  feed surfaces.

## 3. Open-Source / Open-Infrastructure Progress

- OpenAlex and OpenReview create a stronger research signal layer than existed
  even a few years ago.
- GH Archive makes it possible to observe open technical activity as a market
  signal in its own right.
- GDELT broadens Peras beyond AI-only channels into global narrative and event
  monitoring.
- Hugging Face and benchmark archives let Peras watch model-system diffusion
  across communities.

## 4. What This Means For Peras

Peras should not compete on raw ingestion alone.

It should compete on:

- source architecture
- ranking methodology
- judgment object design
- revision discipline over time
