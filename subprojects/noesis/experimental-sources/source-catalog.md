# LOW-TRUST SOURCE CATALOG

- `TRUST_LEVEL: LOW`
- `USE: BRAINSTORMING ONLY`
- `NOT_FOR: DIRECT EVIDENCE OR DECISION MAKING`
- `REQUIRES: INDEPENDENT HIGH-QUALITY CONFIRMATION`

This file lists lower-trust but potentially high-idea-value sources for
Noesis.

They are useful for:

- idea generation
- scenario stimulation
- finding new angles or neglected questions

They are not sufficient for:

- final claims
- governance recommendations
- serious evidence-backed argumentation

## Independent Longform Magazines And Essay Surfaces

- `Noema`
  - URL: https://www.noemamag.com/
  - Why useful: strong essays on technology, philosophy, democracy, capital, and the human future
  - Risk note: high editorial quality but still an essay magazine, not a formal empirical authority

- `Aeon`
  - URL: https://aeon.co/
  - Why useful: broad, conceptually rich essays across philosophy, cognition, society, and human meaning
  - Risk note: good for conceptual stimulation, not a substitute for formal literature review

- `Asterisk`
  - URL: https://asteriskmag.com/
  - Why useful: AI, science, culture, policy, and Bay Area subculture framing in one place
  - Risk note: highly editorial and scene-shaped; useful for discourse mapping, not evidence

- `Works in Progress`
  - URL: https://worksinprogress.news/
  - Why useful: thoughtful essays on institutional design, growth, science, policy, and technology
  - Risk note: often strong, but still synthesis-heavy and not a primary research venue

- `The Gradient`
  - URL: https://thegradient.pub/
  - Why useful: accessible AI essays that often bridge technical and social implications
  - Risk note: strong contributor pool, but still commentary / synthesis rather than a journal

- `Asimov Press`
  - URL: https://www.asimov.press/
  - Why useful: good for scientific and philosophical speculation around the future of knowledge and biology
  - Risk note: can be very stimulating, but speculative and editorial by design

- `Palladium Magazine`
  - URL: https://www.palladiummag.com/
  - Why useful: elite-power, institution, and civilizational arguments relevant to AGI governance scenarios
  - Risk note: ideologically loaded; useful as an ideological signal, not neutral evidence

## Independent Newsletters, Blogs, And Commentary

- `Astral Codex Ten`
  - URL: https://www.astralcodexten.com/
  - Why useful: wide-angle reasoning across cognition, AI, institutions, psychology, and futures
  - Risk note: valuable for idea generation, but strongly author-shaped and not peer reviewed

- `LessWrong`
  - URL: https://www.lesswrong.com/
  - Why useful: concentrated discourse on AI risk, agency, value, rationality, and future scenarios
  - Risk note: community epistemics are uneven; must not be treated as direct evidence

- `Effective Altruism Forum`
  - URL: https://forum.effectivealtruism.org/
  - Why useful: captures long-termist and AI-governance arguments before they hit more formal venues
  - Risk note: agenda-shaped and self-reinforcing; useful as discourse material, not proof

- `Marginal Revolution`
  - URL: https://marginalrevolution.com/
  - Why useful: fast-moving commentary on economics, institutions, technology, and human behavior
  - Risk note: useful for angle discovery, but too blog-like for direct reliance

- `Interconnects`
  - URL: https://www.interconnects.ai/
  - Why useful: independent AI analysis that often bridges technical developments and broader interpretation
  - Risk note: single-author viewpoint and variable empirical depth

- `Simon Willison's Weblog`
  - URL: https://simonwillison.net/
  - Why useful: fast interpretation of agent/tool/runtime changes that can shape human-AI interaction
  - Risk note: strong practical lens, but not a research authority on social evolution

## Policy / Governance / Legal Commentary

- `Lawfare`
  - URL: https://www.lawfaremedia.org/
  - Why useful: timely AI policy, national security, governance, and institutional commentary
  - Risk note: useful interpretive layer, but not neutral or exhaustive

- `Tech Policy Press`
  - URL: https://techpolicy.press/
  - Why useful: broad policy writing on AI, governance, labor, and social systems
  - Risk note: mixed article quality; should be filtered aggressively

- `Bennett Institute for Public Policy blogs and commentary`
  - URL: https://www.bennettinstitute.cam.ac.uk/blog/
  - Why useful: public-policy framing that sometimes catches institutional adaptation themes early
  - Risk note: commentary layer, not itself the evidence base

## Podcasts / Interview Surfaces

- `Dwarkesh Podcast`
  - URL: https://www.dwarkesh.com/
  - Why useful: long-form interviews with frontier researchers and thinkers
  - Risk note: interviews are highly stimulating but not structured evidence

- `80,000 Hours Podcast`
  - URL: https://80000hours.org/podcast/
  - Why useful: long-form conversations on AI risk, governance, and long-term futures
  - Risk note: agenda-shaped and heavily filtered through host priorities

- `Latent.Space`
  - URL: https://www.latent.space/
  - Why useful: sometimes useful for how technical agent language spills into future-of-work and social narratives
  - Risk note: engineering-community perspective, not a civilizational evidence source

## Community / Social Signal Surfaces

- `Hacker News`
  - URL: https://news.ycombinator.com/
  - Why useful: early signal of what builders and operators find salient
  - Risk note: popularity and novelty biases are severe

- `Reddit r/singularity`
  - URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/singularity/
  - Why useful: captures public speculative narratives around AGI futures and human replacement fears
  - Risk note: very noisy and emotionally loaded

- `Reddit r/Futurology`
  - URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/Futurology/
  - Why useful: broad public imagination surface for technology and social futures
  - Risk note: highly unreliable for direct claims

- `Reddit r/MachineLearning`
  - URL: https://www.reddit.com/r/MachineLearning/
  - Why useful: useful for seeing how research and product discourse are being socially interpreted
  - Risk note: mixed quality, highly uneven moderation of claims

## Labor / Society / Cultural Interpretation

- `Platformer`
  - URL: https://www.platformer.news/
  - Why useful: tracks tech-company power, media narratives, and social platform shifts
  - Risk note: journalism and commentary, not a primary research source

- `New Models`
  - URL: https://www.newmodels.io/
  - Why useful: useful for weird-edge cultural and networked-social interpretations of technology
  - Risk note: intentionally interpretive and experimental

- `Ribbonfarm`
  - URL: https://www.ribbonfarm.com/
  - Why useful: long-form social and institutional pattern thinking
  - Risk note: highly author-shaped and often speculative

## Working Rule

If a future Noesis note cites any source from this catalog, the note must
explicitly say:

- this source is inspirational or heuristic
- this source is not treated as direct evidence
- the claim still requires confirmation from higher-trust sources
