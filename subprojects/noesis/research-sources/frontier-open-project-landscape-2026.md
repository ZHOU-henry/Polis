# Noesis Frontier Open-Project Landscape 2026

This file tracks open projects and open infrastructures most relevant to the
Noesis agenda.

## 1. Deliberation And Institutional Coordination

### Pol.is

- Link: <https://github.com/compdemocracy/polis>
- Core idea:
  large-scale open-ended public feedback and clustering without forcing users
  into binary polling formats
- Why Noesis cares:
  directly relevant to communicative rationality, discourse aggregation, and
  post-price coordination models

### Decidim

- Link: <https://github.com/decidim/decidim>
- Core idea:
  participatory democracy framework for proposals, assemblies, voting, and
  institutional deliberation
- Why Noesis cares:
  useful reference point for machine-assisted institutional participation

### CONSUL DEMOCRACY

- Link: <https://github.com/consuldemocracy/consuldemocracy>
- Core idea:
  open-government participation software originally built for Madrid
- Why Noesis cares:
  shows what real civic coordination infrastructure looks like in practice

## 2. Data Governance And Protected Data Collaboration

### PySyft / OpenMined

- Link: <https://github.com/OpenMined/PySyft>
- Core idea:
  enable data science and machine learning on data you are not allowed to see
  directly
- Why Noesis cares:
  useful for thinking about AI-era data ownership, access, and legitimacy

## 3. Energy And Infrastructure Observability

### Electricity Maps

- Link: <https://github.com/electricitymaps/electricitymaps-contrib>
- Core idea:
  real-time and historical visualization of electricity data using public and
  official sources
- Why Noesis cares:
  strongest existing reference for the energy factor inside the six-factor
  observatory

### GH Archive

- Link: <https://www.gharchive.org/>
- Core idea:
  archive the public GitHub timeline for analysis
- Why Noesis cares:
  useful for measuring open technical activity, ecosystem motion, and parts of
  the algorithms / talent factor

## 4. Open Research And Open Ecosystems

### OpenAlex

- Link: <https://openalex.org/>
- Core idea:
  open global catalog of scholarly works, authors, institutions, and concepts
- Why Noesis cares:
  a strong backbone for tracking research movement, institutional clustering,
  and topic-level shifts

### LAION

- Link: <https://laion.ai/>
- Core idea:
  open-scale data and model ecosystem for AI
- Why Noesis cares:
  highly relevant to the data factor, open AI infrastructure politics, and
  concentration vs diffusion dynamics

### Hugging Face Hub

- Link: <https://huggingface.co/>
- Core idea:
  open distribution layer for models, datasets, and evaluation assets
- Why Noesis cares:
  useful for observing algorithm diffusion, dataset ecosystems, and model
  democratization dynamics

## 5. What This Means For Noesis

The open landscape suggests three things:

1. The AI transition is already generating institutional software, not only
   theoretical debate.
2. The six-factor substrate is observable enough to prototype a serious
   research dashboard.
3. Communicative and legitimacy-oriented economic coordination is not a fantasy
   topic if it is grounded in real civic and data infrastructures.
