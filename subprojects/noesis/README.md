# Noesis - AGI Symbiosis Observatory

Open-source scaffold for studying human-machine symbiosis, institutional
adaptation, and social evolution in the AGI era.

Chinese shorthand: `人机共生观测`

## Portfolio Position

Noesis is now one of the three subprojects inside `Polis`, Duckermind's
umbrella program for intelligence economy and coordination systems.

Inside Polis:

- `Noesis` owns the symbiosis, institution, and social-evolution observatory
- `Agora` owns the market and delivery layer
- `Peras` owns the frontier signal and judgment layer

## Stage

Stage 1: project charter, research lanes, and MVP research-program framing.

## Core Question

If AGI-class systems become durable civilizational actors, how should humans,
machine intelligence, and social institutions co-evolve instead of collapsing
into either naive replacement or naive control fantasies?

## Goals

- build a serious research program around human-machine symbiosis in the AGI era
- model the interaction between human agency, machine capability, and social institutions
- separate evidence, inference, design proposals, and speculation clearly
- produce durable frameworks for governance, education, work, culture, and civilizational adaptation

## Program Architecture

Noesis should act as the umbrella research program for five tightly connected layers:

- umbrella layer:
  human-machine symbiosis, institutional adaptation, and AGI-era social evolution
- subproject A:
  artificial consciousness and machine subjectivity research
- subproject B:
  computation boundaries and the limits / structure of machine cognition
- subproject C:
  AI-economy material substrate and six-factor systems research
- subproject D:
  communicative rationality and AI-era economic coordination research

The umbrella question is larger than either subproject alone.

The reason to keep both subprojects inside Noesis is that:

- the symbiosis problem cannot be understood without asking what machine minds are
  or are not
- the symbiosis problem also cannot be understood without asking what machines
  can or cannot compute, learn, or optimize
- the symbiosis problem also cannot be understood without asking how data,
  compute, energy, storage, algorithms, and talent shape the real machine
  economy beneath AGI-era society

## Direction

Noesis is not a generic philosophy repo and not a sci-fi notes folder.

It should become a disciplined observatory and design program for:

- human-machine co-agency
- institutional redesign
- labor and meaning in the AGI era
- capability asymmetry and power concentration
- long-run social evolution under machine abundance
- artificial consciousness and subjectivity questions as one internal subprogram
- computational-boundary and complexity questions as another internal subprogram
