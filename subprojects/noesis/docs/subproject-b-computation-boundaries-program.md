# Subproject B - Computation Boundaries And Machine Limits

## Focus

Study the formal, practical, and structural limits that constrain machine
cognition, and how those limits shape the future division of labor between
humans and machine systems.

## Core Disciplines

- computability theory
- complexity theory
- theoretical computer science
- artificial intelligence
- philosophy of computation
- cognitive science where relevant

## Open Questions

- which machine limits are merely current engineering bottlenecks and which are structural?
- how should we think about "effective" limits in real-world systems versus formal limits in theory?
- where do context-dependence, ambiguity, and social embedment make machine performance brittle?
- which human capacities remain hard to industrialize even under powerful models?
- how should formal limits shape institutional design in an AGI era?

## Agenda

### Track 1 - Formal Boundaries

- computability
- intractability
- approximation limits
- complexity cliffs

### Track 2 - Practical Limits

- robustness
- distribution shift
- tool-use fragility
- multi-agent instability

### Track 3 - Human Comparative Position

- judgment
- interpretation
- norm formation
- context integration

## First Outputs

- machine-limit taxonomy for AGI-era policy and social design
- map of formal vs practical bottlenecks
- memo on where human indispensability may persist even under strong AI
