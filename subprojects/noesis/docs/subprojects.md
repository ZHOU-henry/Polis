# Subprojects

Noesis should explicitly contain four major subprojects under the umbrella of
AGI-era human-machine symbiosis.

## Subproject A

### Artificial Consciousness And Machine Subjectivity

Primary question:

could sufficiently advanced machine systems become more than powerful tools and
instead become entities with mind-like, subject-like, or consciousness-relevant
properties?

Core disciplines:

- neuroscience
- consciousness science
- philosophy of mind
- artificial intelligence
- computer science

Why it matters to the umbrella:

- rights and status questions
- governance design
- moral uncertainty under AGI
- the difference between coordinating tools and coordinating non-human agents

## Subproject B

### Computation Boundaries And Machine Limits

Primary question:

what formal, practical, and structural limits constrain machine cognition, and
how do those limits shape the future division of labor between humans and
machines?

Core disciplines:

- computer science
- computability theory
- complexity theory
- artificial intelligence
- philosophy
- neuroscience and consciousness science where relevant

Why it matters to the umbrella:

- prevents unlimited-machine assumptions from contaminating social design
- clarifies where humans remain strategically necessary
- helps explain where AGI capability may stall, plateau, or distort

## Structural Note

Subproject B belongs inside the Noesis umbrella as a research direction.

That does not require any hard repository dependency.

The important point is conceptual:

- Noesis must contain the machine-limit question
- regardless of how many independent repos also explore adjacent themes

## Subproject C

### AI Economy Material Substrate

Primary question:

what material and economic substrate makes AGI-era society possible, and how do
those six factors shape power, access, and social evolution?

Core axes:

- data
- storage
- compute
- algorithms
- energy
- talent

Why it matters to the umbrella:

- symbiosis is not abstract; it is instantiated through infrastructure and
  economic concentration
- institutional redesign depends on who controls the substrate
- social outcomes depend on bottlenecks and asymmetries in those six factors

## Subproject D

### Communicative Rationality And AI-Era Economic Coordination

Primary question:

how should high-intelligence societies coordinate economic life when prices,
institutions, algorithmic systems, and public legitimacy all interact more
tightly than before?

Core disciplines:

- social theory
- political economy
- economic sociology
- philosophy
- AI governance
- mechanism design where relevant

Primary reference frame:

- Jurgen Habermas on communicative rationality
- discourse legitimacy
- public reason and institutional coordination

Working thesis:

Noesis should study whether parts of the future economy need to move beyond
pure price signaling and pure bureaucratic planning toward a more explicitly
communicative coordination model, especially once AI systems can represent,
simulate, and negotiate social claims at scale.

Why it matters to the umbrella:

- AGI-era society is not only a capability problem but a coordination problem
- institutions require legitimacy, not only efficiency
- AI may increase the feasible bandwidth for social deliberation, preference
  aggregation, and negotiated planning
- economic design in the AI era may need stronger bridges between market
  signals, democratic legitimacy, and machine-assisted coordination
