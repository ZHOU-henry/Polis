# Scenario Brief 03 - Cognitive Stratification And Meaning Inequality

## Status

Working scenario brief.

## Scenario

AGI does not create one uniform labor transition.

Instead, it creates cognitive stratification:

- some people gain access to powerful machine cognition and become amplified
- some become supervisors of machine systems
- some are displaced from decision-heavy work but not fully replaced
- some lose both economic role and social meaning

## Why This Scenario Matters

A symbiosis program that ignores status, dignity, and meaning will be too thin
to describe the real social transition.

## Evidence Inputs

- OECD and future-of-work materials show uneven labor exposure
- company economic framing already treats transition as a workforce redesign
- current AI product deployment is clustered in cognitively intensive sectors

## Inference

The AGI era may create not only productivity inequality, but meaning inequality:

- unequal access to machine amplification
- unequal authority over machine systems
- unequal dignity in the redivision of labor

## Main Risk

- a new cognitive class system
- concentration of social agency among machine-augmented elites
- civic instability if broad populations lose both influence and meaningful role

## Open Questions

- what social designs preserve dignity under machine abundance?
- what education systems are needed for broad cognitive agency?
- can institutions avoid treating large human populations as administratively managed surplus?
