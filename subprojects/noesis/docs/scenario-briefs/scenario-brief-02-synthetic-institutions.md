# Scenario Brief 02 - Synthetic Institutions And Machine Governance

## Status

Working scenario brief.

## Scenario

AGI-era society increasingly relies on synthetic institutions:

- machine-mediated compliance systems
- automated organizational memory
- decision-routing layers
- AI-assisted public and enterprise administration

These systems are not just tools used by institutions.

They start becoming part of what the institution is.

## Why This Scenario Matters

The social effect of AGI may arrive more through institutional transformation
than through visibly autonomous robot-like systems.

## Evidence Inputs

- frontier labs increasingly frame agents as workflow and delegation systems
- enterprise product direction emphasizes copilots, automation, orchestration,
  and review layers
- economic and future-of-work materials already point toward system-level labor
  reconfiguration rather than only task-level augmentation

## Inference

The first deep AGI-era governance challenge may be:

- how to govern institutions whose cognition is partly machine-mediated
- how to preserve legitimate accountability when institutional reasoning is
  distributed across humans and AI layers

## Main Risk

- responsibility diffusion
- institutional opacity
- procedural legitimacy collapse
- quiet transfer of civic and organizational power into vendor-controlled systems

## Open Questions

- what counts as a legitimate institution once core cognition is delegated?
- how much human comprehension is necessary for valid institutional oversight?
- when does a machine layer become constitutive rather than assistive?
