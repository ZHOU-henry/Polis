# Scenario Brief 01 - Cognitive Infrastructure And Human Oversight

## Status

Working scenario brief.

## Scenario

AGI-era society does not first transform through humanoid robots or singular
machine minds.

It transforms through cognitive infrastructure:

- language-model agents
- workflow delegates
- machine research assistants
- enterprise and public-sector decision layers

Humans increasingly do not operate alone. They operate through machine-mediated
cognitive infrastructure.

## Why This Scenario Matters

This is the most plausible near-to-medium transition path because it is already
compatible with:

- enterprise software adoption
- AI copilot framing
- labor-market transition evidence
- current frontier model deployment patterns

## Evidence Inputs

- Stanford HAI AI Index:
  rapid growth in AI capability and adoption framing
- OECD Future of Work:
  pressure on labor systems and institutional adaptation
- OpenAI Economic Blueprint and Anthropic Economic Futures framing:
  explicit focus on workforce and economic transition
- frontier lab product direction:
  increasing emphasis on agents, copilots, and collaborative AI

## Inference

The first large-scale symbiosis regime is likely to look less like "humans versus
AGI" and more like:

- humans embedded in machine-mediated institutions
- machine systems becoming the default substrate for cognition-intensive work
- social power shifting toward whoever controls the best cognitive infrastructure

## Main Risk

The risk is not only labor displacement.

It is also:

- dependency without comprehension
- institutional opacity
- power concentration in cognitive infrastructure providers
- erosion of human oversight capacity

## Open Questions

- what level of human understanding is necessary for legitimate oversight?
- when does delegation become dependency?
- what new public institutions are needed once cognition becomes infrastructural?
