# Subproject C - Six-Factor Observatory Spec

## Goal

Turn the six AI-economy production factors into a measurable observatory rather
than leaving them as a purely conceptual framework.

## Factors

- compute
- algorithms
- data
- storage
- energy
- AI talent

## Product Direction

The observatory should eventually support:

- real-time indicators where feasible
- periodic slice snapshots where real-time data is not feasible
- country, company, and region comparisons
- trend charts
- concentration maps
- cross-factor interaction analysis

## Why This Matters

The AI economy is increasingly shaped by the distribution of these six factors.

Without measurement, Noesis risks staying at the level of elegant theory.

With measurement, Noesis can support:

- AI economics research
- AGI-era social and industrial analysis
- infrastructure concentration studies
- policy and governance work
- comparative global development analysis

## Example Data Directions

### Compute

- hyperscaler capex
- data-center announcements
- cluster scale
- GPU supply and deployment signals

### Algorithms

- model releases
- benchmark movement
- architecture diffusion
- training-method adoption

### Data

- major dataset releases
- data partnerships
- synthetic-data usage
- data-governance changes

### Storage

- data-center storage buildout
- cloud storage pricing and supply signals
- enterprise data infrastructure movement

### Energy

- power agreements
- grid constraints
- nuclear / renewable tie-ins for AI infrastructure
- regional power bottlenecks

### AI Talent

- hiring concentration
- migration patterns
- compensation signals
- key-lab and key-company talent clusters

## Output Forms

- six-factor scoreboard
- regional comparison cards
- time-series trend panels
- concentration alerts
- narrative memos on major shifts

## Constraint

Not every factor will support the same cadence or precision.

The observatory should separate:

- direct measured indicators
- derived indicators
- inference-based indicators

so the research stays honest about signal quality.
