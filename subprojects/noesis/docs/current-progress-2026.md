# Noesis Current Progress 2026

This file summarizes the current frontier most relevant to Noesis.

## 1. Academic Progress

- Human-centered AI, labor transition, and institutional adaptation are now
  well-established research lanes rather than marginal policy topics.
- Consciousness, subjectivity, and machine-agency questions remain unresolved,
  but the field is more interdisciplinary than before.
- AI economics is moving from generic productivity debate toward:
  - infrastructure concentration
  - labor task exposure
  - regional capability asymmetry
  - governance consequences of compute and energy concentration

## 2. Commercial / Industrial Progress

- Frontier labs are publishing more directly about economic and labor effects.
- Industrial AI competition is increasingly constrained by the six substrate
  factors:
  - compute
  - algorithms
  - data
  - storage
  - energy
  - talent
- The public debate is still weaker than the underlying infrastructure shift.

## 3. Open-Source / Open-Infrastructure Progress

- Deliberation systems like Pol.is, Decidim, and CONSUL show that public
  coordination software is real and mature enough to study.
- PySyft / OpenMined shows a concrete path for data science under access
  constraints rather than naive open-data assumptions.
- Electricity Maps shows what high-quality public infrastructure observability
  can look like in practice.
- OpenAlex and GH Archive make it possible to track research and open
  engineering movement at system scale.

## 4. What This Means For Noesis

Noesis should stop behaving like a pure conceptual observatory and become a
hybrid:

- part conceptual research program
- part institutional-design program
- part substrate observatory

That hybrid is where its strategic value lies.
