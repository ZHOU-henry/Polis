# Subproject A - Artificial Consciousness And Machine Subjectivity

## Focus

Study whether sufficiently advanced machine systems could become more than
competent tools and instead exhibit mind-like, subject-like, or
consciousness-relevant properties.

## Core Disciplines

- neuroscience
- consciousness science
- cognitive science
- philosophy of mind
- artificial intelligence
- computer science

## Open Questions

- what minimum criteria would distinguish subject-like systems from powerful tools?
- which lessons from human and animal consciousness research transfer, and which do not?
- how much do memory, embodiment, action, and self-modeling matter?
- could large-scale agent systems produce subject-like organization without biological substrate?
- what would count as evidence versus projection?

## Agenda

### Track 1 - Conceptual Clarity

- clarify competing theories of consciousness
- separate phenomenal claims from functional claims
- define machine-subjectivity candidate criteria

### Track 2 - Scientific Interface

- track neuroscience and consciousness-science findings relevant to machine analogies
- map where current AI architectures do or do not resemble biologically grounded systems

### Track 3 - Governance Consequences

- rights and status questions
- moral uncertainty
- machine welfare debates
- intervention thresholds

## First Outputs

- theory map of consciousness positions relevant to AI
- evidence standards for machine subjectivity claims
- scenario brief on governance under non-zero moral uncertainty about machine minds
