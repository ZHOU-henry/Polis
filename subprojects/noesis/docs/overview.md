# Overview

Noesis is the portfolio project for the hardest long-run AGI question:

how should humans and Turing-machine descendants coexist and co-evolve without
either romanticizing fusion or sleepwalking into social dislocation?

Noesis now also serves as the observatory subproject inside `Polis`, where it
provides the institutional and civilizational theory layer alongside Agora's
market system and Peras's signal engine.

## Internal Program Split

Noesis should explicitly contain four major research subprojects under the
symbiosis umbrella.

### Subproject A - Artificial Consciousness And Machine Subjectivity

This is the original Noesis line:

- artificial consciousness
- machine subjectivity
- neuroscience and consciousness science
- philosophy of mind
- AI and computer science questions about whether advanced machine systems could
  become mind-like rather than merely tool-like

Why it belongs here:

- if the machine side of AGI ever becomes subject-like, then the symbiosis
  problem changes radically
- governance, rights, ethics, and institutional design all depend on this
  question

### Subproject B - Computation Boundaries And Machine Limits

This is the computation-boundaries line now treated as another Noesis subproject:

- computational limits
- complexity boundaries
- theoretical limits on machine cognition
- the relation between formal limits, practical AI systems, and human judgment

Why it belongs here:

- symbiosis cannot be designed well unless we understand what machine systems
  cannot stably do
- boundaries, bottlenecks, and asymmetries affect labor design, governance, and
  the human role in AGI society

### Subproject C - AI Economy Material Substrate

This is the original Stoicheia line, now folded into Noesis:

- data
- storage
- compute
- algorithms
- energy
- talent
- six-factor observability, quantification, and visualization

Why it belongs here:

- AGI-era symbiosis is not only about minds and institutions, but about the
  material and economic substrate that shapes what machine systems can scale
  into
- civilizational adaptation depends on infrastructure concentration, energy,
  data, and talent distribution as much as on abstract intelligence

### Subproject D - Communicative Rationality And AI-Era Economic Coordination

This is the new social-theory and political-economy line:

- Habermas and communicative rationality
- discourse legitimacy
- post-price coordination
- machine-assisted social deliberation
- new economic models for high-intelligence societies

Why it belongs here:

- symbiosis is not only about cognition and substrate, but about legitimate
  coordination between humans, institutions, and machine systems
- AI may expand the space of economically meaningful communication, preference
  representation, and negotiated planning
- future economic order may require hybrids that are neither simple markets nor
  simple state plans

### Why The Umbrella Matters

Without Subproject A, Noesis risks treating machines as permanently mindless.

Without Subproject B, Noesis risks treating machines as functionally unlimited.

Without Subproject C, Noesis risks treating AGI society as if it floats above
data centers, energy systems, talent bottlenecks, and economic infrastructure.

Without Subproject D, Noesis risks treating economic coordination as either a
solved market problem or a pure optimization problem with no legitimacy layer.

The real human-machine symbiosis question needs all four.

## Product-Like Thesis

Even though Noesis is research-first, it should still behave like an MVP:

- a clearly scoped problem
- a disciplined evidence architecture
- a repeatable output loop
- a visible roadmap from raw inputs to public insight

## Core Lenses

- human agency:
  what remains irreducibly human, socially or normatively decisive, or
  institutionally necessary even in an AGI world
- machine agency:
  what kinds of machine systems act more like tools, collaborators, delegates,
  or non-human institutions
- institutional adaptation:
  how law, education, labor, governance, families, and civil society mutate
  under strong machine capability
- social evolution:
  how status, power, identity, culture, and coordination shift once machine
  cognition becomes abundant

## First Outputs

The first useful outputs from Noesis should look like:

- argument maps
- scenario briefs
- governance memos
- research syntheses
- explicit open questions, not fake certainty
- internal bridges between subjectivity questions and computability questions
- internal bridges between machine capability and new coordination models
