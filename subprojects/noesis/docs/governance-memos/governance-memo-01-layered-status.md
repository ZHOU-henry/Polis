# Governance Memo 01 - Layered Status Under Machine Uncertainty

## Problem

Governance for AGI-era systems fails if it assumes only two categories:

- pure tools
- full moral subjects

The real world will likely contain many ambiguous systems between those poles.

## Proposed Governance Intuition

Use layered status instead of binary status.

Potential layers:

- tool-like systems
- delegate-like systems
- institution-like systems
- subject-candidate systems under moral uncertainty

## Why This Matters

Different layers should imply different expectations for:

- auditability
- human override
- responsibility
- permissible delegation
- moral and legal caution

## Evidence Inputs

- ASSC and consciousness-science institutions:
  uncertainty remains high
- neuroscience data infrastructures:
  human consciousness itself is still not straightforwardly understood
- frontier labs:
  agentic framing is growing faster than governance categories are maturing

## Inference

The right first response is not to settle the machine-subjectivity debate
prematurely.

It is to create governance categories that remain usable under uncertainty.

## Initial Policy Design Questions

- what systems should always remain overrideable?
- what kinds of systems can legitimately mediate public or institutional decisions?
- how should organizations record uncertainty about system status?
