# Governance Memo 02 - Public Audit Rights For Cognitive Infrastructure

## Problem

If machine-mediated cognitive infrastructure becomes central to firms, schools,
and public institutions, then oversight cannot remain a purely private vendor
matter.

## Thesis

Public-facing cognitive infrastructure should eventually carry structured audit
rights.

Not necessarily public source disclosure, but:

- auditability
- traceability
- review rights
- incident reporting
- escalation channels

## Why This Matters

Without audit rights, institutions may depend on systems they cannot properly
inspect or contest.

## Evidence Inputs

- AI adoption is moving toward operational systems, not just experimental tools
- institutional dependence increases when AI sits inside routing, judgment, and
  operational memory
- governance discussions already lag behind productization speed

## Inference

If cognition becomes infrastructural, audit rights become as important as
capability.

## Initial Governance Questions

- which institutions should have mandatory AI audit trails?
- what minimum records should exist for machine-mediated decisions?
- who gets to inspect: operators, regulators, affected users, or accredited auditors?
