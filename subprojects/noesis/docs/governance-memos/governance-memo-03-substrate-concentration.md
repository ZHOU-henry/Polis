# Governance Memo 03 - Substrate Concentration And Civilizational Resilience

## Problem

AGI-era society will not run on abstract intelligence alone.

It will run on a material substrate:

- data
- storage
- compute
- algorithms
- energy
- talent

If too much of that substrate concentrates, human-machine symbiosis may become
politically and socially unstable.

## Thesis

AGI governance must treat substrate concentration as a first-class social risk.

## Why This Matters

Even if alignment improves, a highly concentrated substrate can still produce:

- political dependency
- economic lock-in
- civilizational fragility
- asymmetry between regions, institutions, and populations

## Evidence Inputs

- frontier AI is already compute- and talent-concentrated
- energy and data-center dependency are rising
- platform concentration repeatedly amplifies power asymmetry in prior digital waves

## Inference

Symbiosis that depends on a narrow substrate monopoly may look stable in the
short run but brittle in the long run.

## Initial Governance Questions

- which substrate layers most need diversification?
- what resilience metrics should matter more than short-term efficiency?
- how should societies reason about AGI sovereignty without collapsing into techno-nationalism?
