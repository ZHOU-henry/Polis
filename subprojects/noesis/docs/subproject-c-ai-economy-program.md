# Subproject C - AI Economy Material Substrate

## Focus

Study the material and economic substrate of AGI-era society through six
interlocking factors:

- data
- storage
- compute
- algorithms
- energy
- talent

## Why It Belongs Inside Noesis

Human-machine symbiosis is not only a philosophical or institutional question.

It is also constrained by:

- who owns the infrastructure
- where energy can flow
- where talent clusters
- how compute is allocated
- how data is governed

Without this substrate layer, AGI social theory becomes detached from reality.

## Open Questions

- which of the six factors will become the hardest bottleneck in an AGI era?
- how do these factors interact with labor, education, governance, and power concentration?
- what kinds of institutional redesign become necessary when the substrate is highly centralized?
- how should societies think about resilience, sovereignty, and access across these six factors?

## Agenda

### Track 1 - Factor Maps

- define each factor precisely
- map pairwise interaction between factors
- identify likely concentration points

### Track 2 - Institutional Consequences

- labor effects
- geopolitical effects
- industrial concentration
- governance implications

### Track 3 - Social Evolution Relevance

- how substrate concentration changes human autonomy
- how energy / compute / data shape the real symbiosis frontier

### Track 4 - Six-Factor Intelligence Observatory

- collect real-time and sliced internet data for:
  - compute
  - algorithms
  - data
  - storage
  - energy
  - AI talent
- define quantitative indicators for each factor
- build comparative time-series and regional slices
- visualize concentration, growth, bottlenecks, and cross-factor interaction
- support both Noesis research and broader AGI-era economic analysis

## Research Boundary Expansion

Subproject C should not remain only a conceptual political-economy program.

It should also become a measurement and observability program for the AI
economy.

That means treating the six factors as:

- research concepts
- measurable variables
- continuously updated public indicators
- inputs to future visualization and dashboard systems

## Data Ambition

The long-run goal is a six-factor observatory that can:

- ingest global internet data
- maintain historical slices
- quantify relative concentration and momentum
- expose charts and structured comparisons
- help explain AGI-era economic direction in a more empirical way

This is especially important for:

- industrial strategy
- governance analysis
- infrastructure concentration
- talent migration
- energy and compute bottlenecks
- global AI capability asymmetry

## First Outputs

- six-factor argument map
- AGI substrate risk memo
- comparative framework for infrastructure concentration and social outcomes
- observatory specification for six-factor data collection and visualization
